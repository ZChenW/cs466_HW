\paragraph*{Problem 1.} (50 points.)
Define key-generation algorithm $\calK$ to output a random 128-bit key
$K$ and define encryption algorithm $\calE$ by
\begin{center}
\begin{minipage}{1.6in}
\begin{tabbing}
123\=123\=123\=123\=123\=\kill
\textbf{Algorithm} $\calE_K(M)$: \\
\> $C[0] \getsr \bits^{128}$ \\
\> For $i = 1$ to $m$ do: \\
\>\> $W[i] \gets \langle (C[0] + i) \bmod 2^{128} \rangle$ \\
\>\> $C[i] \gets \mathsf{AES}_K(M[i] \oplus W[i])$ \\
\> $C \gets C[0] \| \ldots \| C[m]$ \\
\> Return $C$
\end{tabbing}
\end{minipage}
\end{center}
Above we parse $M$ as consisting of $m$ blocks of 128 bits each, and
$\langle \cdot \rangle$ denotes the standard 128-bit integer encoding.
Note that this scheme can be seen as a variant of counter mode. Recall that standard CTR mode computes
$C[i] \gets \mathsf{AES}_K\!\bigl(\langle(R + i) \bmod 2^{128}\rangle\bigr) \oplus M[i]$:
the key stream is generated by applying $\mathsf{AES}$ to a counter derived from
the nonce $R$, and the message block is XOR'd with the \emph{output} of $\mathsf{AES}$.
In the scheme above the roles are reversed: the message block is XOR'd with the
counter \emph{before} being fed into $\mathsf{AES}$, so that the input to $\mathsf{AES}$
is message-dependent.

\medskip\noindent (Part A --- 20 points.)
Define a decryption algorithm $\calD$ such that
$\schemefont{SE} = (\calK, \calE, \calD)$ is a correct symmetric-key
encryption scheme.

\medskip\noindent (Part B --- 80 points.)
Show that $\schemefont{SE}$ is \emph{not} IND-CPA.
Your adversary must be practical: it should make at most a small constant
number of $\LR$-queries and perform only minor additional computation.
State your adversary in pseudocode and compute its advantage and resource usage.
