\paragraph*{Acronyms.} Below,  EKS means exhaustive key search, CKR means conistent key recovery, TKR mean target key recovery.  Please ask me if anything is unclear. 

\paragraph*{Problem 1.} Define the family of functions $F \colon \bits^{256} \times \bits^{256} \to \bits^{256}$ by 
 \begin{center}
\begin{minipage}{1in}
\begin{tabbing}
123\=123\=123\=123\=123\=\kill
\textbf{Algorithm} $F_{K_1 \| K_2}(x_1 \| x_2)$: \\
\> Return $\mathsf{AES}^{-1}(K_1, x_1 \oplus x_2) \| \mathsf{AES}(K_2, \overline{x_2})$
\end{tabbing} \end{minipage}
\end{center}
for all $K_1, K_2, x_1, x_2 \in \bits^{128}$. Here  `$\|$' denotes string concatenation, `$\oplus$' denotes bit-wise exclusive-or, and $\overline{x}$ denotes the bit-wise complement of a string $x$.  Let $T_{\mathsf{AES}}$ denote the time for one computation of $\mathsf{AES}$ or $\mathsf{AES}^{-1}$. Below, running-times are worst case and should be functions of $T_{\mathsf{AES}}$. \\\\
 (Part A - 10 points.) Prove that $F$ is a blockcipher. \\\\
 (Part B - 10 points.) What is the running-time of a 4-query EKS adversary  against $F$? \\\\
 (Part C - 30 points.) Give the most efficient 4-query CKR adversary that you can having advantage 1 against $F$. State your adversary in  pseudocode and  analyze its advantage and resource usage. To get any points, you need to substantially beat EKS.

 \paragraph*{Problem 2.} 

For $n \in \N$, define the family of functions  $$\mathsf{OTP} \colon \bits^n\times\bits^n\to\bits^n$$ by $\mathsf{OTP}_K(x)= K\oplus x$ for all $K,x\in \bits^n$.
Now define another family of functions $$\mathsf{OTP}':\bits^{2n}\times\bits^n\to\bits^n$$ where a key $K \in \bits^{2n}$ is parsed as $K_1 \| K_2$ for $K_1,K_2 \in \bits^n$ and 
$$\mathsf{OTP}'_{K_1\|K_2}(x) = \mathsf{OTP}_{K_1}(x)$$ for all $K_1,K_2,x \in \bits^n$. \\\\
(Part A - 25 points.) Give an efficient 1-query CKR adversary with maximal advantage against $\mathsf{OTP}'$. As usual, state your adversary in  pseudocode and  analyze its advantage and resource usage. To get any points, you need to substantially beat EKS. \\\\
(Part B - 25 points.) Give an efficient 1-query TKR adversary with maximal advantage against $\mathsf{OTP}'$. As usual, state your adversary in  pseudocode and  analyze its advantage and resource usage. To get any points, you need to substantially beat EKS.
