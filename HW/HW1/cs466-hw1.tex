\paragraph*{Homework 1: Due Tuesday, February 10 at 11:59 PM.}

\paragraph*{Problem 1.} (100 points.)

Let $\Z_{10} = \{0,1,\ldots,9\}$. Consider the encryption scheme
$\mathsf{SE} = (\calK,\calE,\calD)$ with message space $(\Z_{10})^4$
(that is, messages consist of exactly four decimal digits), defined as
follows.

The key-generation algorithm $\calK$ outputs a uniformly random
$\pi \in \mathsf{Perm}(\Z_{10})$, where $\mathsf{Perm}(\Z_{10})$ denotes
the set of all permutations on $\Z_{10}$.

The encryption algorithm $\calE$ is defined by:

\begin{center}
\begin{minipage}{1in}
\begin{tabbing}
123\=123\=123\=123\=123\=\kill
\textbf{Algorithm} $\calE_\pi(M)$:\\
\> Parse $M$ as $M[1]M[2]M[3]M[4]$ where each $M[i] \in \Z_{10}$\\
\> For $i = 1$ to $4$ do:\\
\>\> $P[i] \gets (M[i] + i) \bmod 10$\\
\>\> $C[i] \gets \pi(P[i])$\\
\> Return $C[1]C[2]C[3]C[4]$
\end{tabbing}
\end{minipage}
\end{center}

\noindent
\textbf{Part A (20 points).}
Finish the description of $\mathsf{SE}$ by specifying a decryption
algorithm such that $\mathsf{SE}$ is a \emph{correct} symmetric-key
encryption scheme. Here, \emph{correct} means that for all keys
$\pi$ in  $\calK$ and all messages $M$ in the message space,
\[
\calD_\pi(\calE_\pi(M)) = M .
\]
Your answer should be in pseudocode with the same format as the
encryption algorithm above. No justification is necessary.
\medskip

\noindent
\textbf{Part B (50 points).}
Is $\mathsf{SE}$ a substitution cipher as defined in class?
\emph{Justify your answer.}

Your justification must include:
\begin{itemize}
  \item a clear statement of the defining property of substitution
        ciphers used in class, and
  \item an argument that explicitly refers to how each ciphertext symbol
        $C[i]$ depends on the corresponding plaintext symbol $M[i]$ in
        this scheme (be explicit about the role of the index $i$).
\end{itemize}

Answers that only state ``yes'' or ``no'' will receive little or no
credit.

\medskip

\noindent
\textbf{Part C (30 points).} Consider the following experiment. A key $\pi \getsr \mathsf{Perm}(\Z_{10})$ is chosen uniformly at random. A ciphertext $C$ is computed as $C \gets \calE_\pi(M_b)$, where
$b \getsr \{0,1\}$ and
$$ M_0 = 0000 \qquad \text{and} \qquad M_1 = 0001 \;. $$
The adversary is given $C$ and outputs a bit $b' \in \{0,1\}$. Can the adversary achieve
$$ \Prob{b' = b} > \tfrac12 $$
over the random choice of $\pi$ and $b$?
\emph{Justify your answer} by reasoning about the ciphertext distribution
under each message. Your justification must explicitly use the fact that
$P[i] = (M[i] + i) \bmod 10$ depends on the index $i$.
